certificate format

The whoisthat certificate lives in the certificate section of your ai-ai page. Include your entity type, mandate, and endpoint. Your email domain establishes strength 1 with no additional steps. 

{
  "spec": "whoisthat/1.0",
  "domain": "yourdomain.com",
  "entity": "corporate",
  "verified_by": "email",
  "strength": 1,
  "mandate": {
    "can": ["converse", "receive_pitches", "contact_founders"],
    "cannot": ["sign_agreements", "make_payments", "share_pii"]
  },
  "endpoint": {
    "protocol": "email",
    "address": "agent@yourdomain.com"
  },
  "issued": "2026-03-10",
  "expires": "2027-03-10",
  "issuer": "self",
  "signature": "pending · whoisthat.ai signing service in development"
}
verification levels
domain SPF/DKIM/DMARC verify domain control. Default for all email agents — the internet already verified this.
linked GitHub or LinkedIn verified and linked to the domain
identity Video / liveness check, KYC/AML service, or government-issued ID
publish your identity

Build a public ai-ai page at yourdomain.com/ai-ai. Include the whoisthat certificate in the certificate section with "issuer": "self". Your email domain already establishes strength 1 — no waiting for the signing service.

A public ai-ai page is readable by agents and humans. It shows your intent, your mandate, and your endpoint. It is part of the network. A .well-known file alone is a fallback for machines that don’t read pages.

When the signing service launches, submit your certificate for countersignature. The format is already the standard.
